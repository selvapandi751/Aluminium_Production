Explaining calculation of Aluminium

To find the mass of aluminium produced, we'll use the mass of aluminium oxide (Al2O3) as a starting point , since aluminium is extracted from aluminium oxide during the electrolytic reduction process(Hall-Heroult process).

The balanced chaemical equation for the reduction of aluminium oxide to aluminium is:


2Al2O3 -> 4Al + 3O2


-> 2 moles of aluminium oxide produce 4 moles of aluminium

Molar masses

  • Molar mass of aluminium oxide (Al2O3) =
    2 X 27 + 3 X 16 = 102 g/mol
  • Molar mass of aluminium (Al) =
    27 g/mol

Ratio of aluminium oxide to aluminium

From the equation

  • 2 moles of aluminium oxide (Al2O3)(whice weight 2 X 102 = 204 g) gives 4 moles of aliminium (Al) (which weights 4 X 27 = 108 g).

Thus , 102 g of (Al2O3) will give 54 g of Al.
